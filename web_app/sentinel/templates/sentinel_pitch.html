What Sentinel does

It turns "does this site feel right?" into a repeatable test.

A user gives Sentinel a URL, scenario, device profile, and demographic. Sentinel opens a real browser, sees the page multimodally, acts like a customer, captures screenshots, and returns a concise report with evidence.

1. Observe

Computer vision over real pages

Annotated screenshots and slim element maps let the agent judge layout, affordance, tone, and navigability without drowning in raw DOM text.

2. Simulate

Synthetic customers at scale

Run checkout, signup, contact, booking, or discovery flows as different devices and customer demographics.

3. Report

Evidence, not gut feel

Each run produces screenshots, step logs, findings, and a final narrative that teams can act on or sell as a service.

The opening

Websites are easier to ship than ever. They are not easier to trust.

Vibe-coding is creating more pages, funnels, and micro-businesses. The bottleneck moves from building to knowing whether an actual customer can understand, trust, and buy from the thing that was just shipped.

Marketability

The value is the sale that does not silently disappear.

The worst case for a business is not a visible bug. It is a buyer who feels confused, distrustful, or blocked, leaves without purchasing, and never tells you why.

Hobbyists and vibe-coders QA without building a QA stack.

They need a fast "vibe-test" for the growing number of sites they can now ship, without sacrificing their own laptop or configuring browsers.

Small businesses Lead generation through automated audits.

Run reports against poorly converting local sites, then sell a concrete improvement plan instead of a vague redesign pitch.

Large businesses Enterprise setup and consulting.

Install in-house Sentinel pipelines for continuous synthetic customer testing across brands, geographies, devices, and regulated flows.

Product direction

From browser agent to synthetic user network.

The long-term wedge is a library of repeatable customer personas and purpose-built multimodal models that are cheaper, faster, and better at specific UX tests than general-purpose agents.
